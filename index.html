History

Halloween is celebrated on the eve of All Hallows' Day, which is dedicated to remembering the day in the Christian calendar. The festival is said to have originated from Gaelic and Welsh folklore and is meant to mark the end of harvest season and the beginning of the darker half of the year.

Now

Over the years halloween tradition has changed drastically and nowadays halloween is considered a more child oriented event. On the deadly night it is common to see many taking part in activities such as trick or treating, dressing up spooky custumes, making halloween related food, scavenger hunts, etc.
